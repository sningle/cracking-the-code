    There are many career paths for people studying computer science, but which one is right for you? Here are some of the most common careers Computer Scientists pursue and what exactly they do. (Note: Many people take non-traditional paths in Computer Science that involve learning coding languages through personal projects rather than a degree program. Thus, degree requirements may be negotiable.)


    Computer and Information Research Scientist

    Summary: Computer and information research scientists design and research various applications for new as well as existing technologies. Specifically, their goal is to improve computer hardware by designing innovative computer architectures which increase the efficiency, speed, or security of exusting technology.

    Education Needed: Master's Degree

    Median Pay: $126,830


    Computer Network Architect

    Summary: Computer network architects create data communication networks which transport data between computers using wired and wireless technology. These networks mainly include local area networks (LANs), wide area networks (WANs), and Intranets which all aid in communication between devices.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $116,780


    Software Developer

    Summary: Software developers design software applications as well as the underlying systems that run each device or control a network. Software developers may work for a company that services many different clients and provides software for many companies or they may work for one company and provide software solely for use within the company.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $110,140


    Infomation Security Analyst

    Summary: Information security analysts fortify networks and implement security measures to protect said networks. Information security analysts also prepare emergency plans to ensure a company’s data is backed up in the event of a disaster.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $103,590


    Database Administrator

    Database administrators design systems that store and protect data. Many database administrators work for companies with large databases such as universities or insurance companies.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $98,860


    Data Scientist

    Data scientists use technological and social science to analyze data and find trends. They use a wealth of knowledge from many different areas to develop solutions to business challenges.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $98,230


    Systems Analyst

    Computer systems analysts study existing computer systems to find ways to make them more efficient or effective.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $93,730


    Full-Stack Web Developer

    Full-stack web developers work on both client-side and server-side software. They're able to program browsers, servers, and databases and require a variety of skills to balance both front end and back end management.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $77,200


    User Interface Designer

    User interface designers focus on designing interfaces that are easy for users to interact with and aesthetically pleasing. This position requires both artistic skill as well as web development skill and focuses on meeting all of a user's needs within the framework of the product.

    Education Needed: Bachelor's Degree

    Median Pay: $65,600
