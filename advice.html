    Looking for tips, tricks, or encouragement? Below, we have words of wisdom for you from women at various stages in their computer science journeys. Find some humor, joy, and hope here!

    "Don't be discouraged because of how vast CS is. Start small and follow along with tutorials on YouTube in order to familiarize yourself with new languages and tools."

    -Abby Nacional

    Computer Science is a wide field! You don't have to know how to code to get into tech industries if you don't like to code. But if you do, then you have many opportunities to put yourself out there! And don't let being a girl make you stop from pursuing this pathway, computer science will be a very good career path!

    -Nour Siwar

    "CS is not just for weird guys who don't go outside! Comp Sci is actually fun and really prevalent in many companies."

    -Somer

    "It's okay to not know everything, and that everyone starts from somewhere. Also start thinking about how you maybe want to apply those skills, like what type of project you want to make which can help when deciding on learning what language or technology to know. Also to seek out mentorship if possible. I for one did not have access to maybe someone who was doing what I hoped to do, but I was able to find mentorship in other people that did not have technical advice per say, but other types of advice that were also valuable. Keep a look out for scholarships and programs. And most importantly, put yourself first and take care of your mental healt; do what you can do and allow yourself to rest."

    -Marcela

    "It can be really easy to give up when you have no one there in your corner. Find your support group, whether it's friends, a classmate, teacher, etc. Don't get discouraged, it's ok to ask for help."

    -Julia

    "Use all the free open resources, network, don’t be afraid to do a hackathon!"

    -Joanne Wenceslao

    "Use all the resources available, watch lots of videos, and find something you’d love to learn to do."

    -Anonymous

    "Computer science is a very large field. I recommend trying everything and anything at first to find which part(s) you find engaging and fun! After you find the "subfield" that you connect most with (be it back-end, front-end, data management, HCI, etc), find projects and internships that specifically work with the subfield. Make sure to present your passions in your interviews to ensure that you get assigned to teams and get assigned work that interests you!"

    -Charlotte Hahn
