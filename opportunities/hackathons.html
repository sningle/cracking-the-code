    • HACKATHONS

    Hackathons are a great way to learn new skills, network with other hackers and mentors, and use what you've learned to build a project on something you're passionate about! In recent years, there has been a rise in hackathons specifically meant for women and non-binary students. Here are some awesome ones you should consider participating in.

    Pearl Hacks

    Pearl Hacks is a hackathon targeted towards women and non-binary college students. The best part is you don't need any coding experience to participate! There are many opportunities to get to know other participants and form teams, and there are a variety of roles which are non-coding focused, such as design or management. If you are interested in learning how to code or improving your coding skills, there are tons of workshops which participants have the opportunity to attend, both in-person and virtually.

    Check it out!

    Technica

    Technica is the world's largest hackathon designed for women and non-binary people. This hackathon is open to hackers of all ages and experiences, so minors and people with no prior coding knowledge are free to participate! In addition to creating hacks for the program's several tracks, participants can also network, attend workshops and mini-events, and hear other people in tech talk about their experiences.

    Check it out!

    Creatica

    Creatica is a virtual hackathon open to all high school and college students, but focuses on supporting women and non-binary people in tech. This year's hackathon centers on ethical tech and offers special prizes to hacks submitted adhering to the ethical tech theme. In addition, the other events at the hackathon, such as the workshops and tech talks, will also revolve around the theme. People of all experience levels are invited to join!

    Check it out!
