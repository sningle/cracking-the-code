    • CODING CAMPS AND OTHER PROGRAMS

    As the tech industry grows bigger and bigger, many organizations have started to offer camps and other programs to pre-teen and teen girls in order to introduce them to a potential future in the field. Here are a few you can take a look at!

    Girls Who Code

    Girls Who Code is an amazing organization for young women in high school wanting to pursue a career in computer science in the future. The organization hosts many events, one of which being a summer immersion program which is free! The program is currently completely virtual and is two weeks long. During the program, participants will have the opportunity to learn web development technologies including HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

    Check it out!

    Black Girls Code

    As the name implies, Black Girls Code is an organization dedicated to making tech more accessible to underrepresented groups, specifically Black girls. The organization's program areas cover web development, artificial intelligence, virtual reality, robotics, and much more. In addition to hackathons, coding clubs, and workshops, BCG hosts summer camps intended for pre-teen and teen girls to attend. The camps are usually two weeks long and focus on a specific area in tech, such as game design.

    Check it out!

    Microsoft DigiGirlz

    Microsoft offers free technology programs for middle and high school girls through Microsoft Digigirlz. These programs include a high tech camp, a hackathon, and Digigirlz Day. Participants are able to explore how broad the tech field is through hands-on sessions making apps, watching tech demonstrations, and hearing directly from industry professionals. Even better, most of these events are available across the globe!

    Check it out!
