    Not everyone has the privilege of attending an expensive university or bootcamp in order to get a job in tech. Financial issues should never be the reason someone is unable to enter this field. There are a variety of financial aid resources specifically for underrepresented genders in tech! Here are a few to get you started.

    Women at Microsoft

    If you are a woman or non-binary individual in high school and need financial assistance to attend college, the Women at Microsoft Scholarship might be for you. Applications are open to high school seniors who want to pursue a career in STEAM (science, technology, engineering, arts, and math). While your intended major doesn't have to be computer science, the scholarship is focused on enabling young women and non-binary people to pursue a career in technology. Winners will receive a $5,000 award which they may be able to renew for up to four consecutive years.

    Check it out!

    Palantir Women in Technology

    The Palantir Women in Technology Scholarship is aimed at women who are already enrolled in college and are pursuing computer science, software engineering, or other related undergraduate degrees. Participants may be part-time students, and can apply no matter where they live in North America. The application process consists of two essay-style responses. Recipients will be granted up to $7,000.

    Check it out!

    Women Take Tech

    If a bootcamp seems more appealing or likely than college, don't worry! Plenty of bootcamps still have scholarships for women and non-binary people trying to break into tech. The Women Take Tech Scholarship by Flatiron School is a great example. Winners will have between $1,000 and $3,000 of their tuition covered, with the possibility of additional grants for those who are low-income. Around 75 women will be awarded this scholarship a year. Applicants must be at least 18 years old.

    Check it out!
